# AD Adoption Model — Notes

A five-level maturity model across seven dimensions for assessing and improving organizational AD practices.

## 5 Adoption Levels

1. **Undefined & Unconscious** — emergent architecture, no ADRs or free-form only
2. **Ad-hoc & Unstructured** — single teams use ADRs after-the-fact, no unified process
3. **Encouraged & Supported** — multiple teams use ADRs, reviews encouraged
4. **Systematic, Selective & Diligent** — hard-to-reverse decisions made systematically, mandatory reviews, central repos
5. **Optimized & Rigorous** — org-wide systematic AD making, AD boards, communities of practice, formal training

## 7 Dimensions

1. Usage scenario (after-the-fact vs. steering design)
2. Scope and scale (local teams vs. enterprise sharing)
3. Structure and location (template rigor, repository searchability)
4. Process and engagement (timing, Definition of Done)
5. Tool support and automation
6. Review culture (optional vs. mandatory)
7. Learning/education (self-organized vs. formal)

## Core Takeaways

- Not all organizations need Level 5 — Level 4 is an ideal practical target for most teams
- Use a spider-web chart across 7 dimensions to assess current vs. desired state
- Start simple (markdown files, peer reviews) before investing in tooling integration
- Progress gradually: document informally → apply templates → get reviews → share → formalize
